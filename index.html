CONCORD

The term 'concord' means 'agreement',looking at the image above we all know what it is to agree,but in this context we will be talking about the agreement between a noun and a verb in a sentence. Concord is the agreement or harmony between different part of a sentence,it is said to be the agreement in number and person between the subject of the sentence and its verb.

Examples

  • The man is my Uncle
  • The men are my Uncles

Looking at the examples stated above you can unavoidably see the agreement between the noun used in each sentences and the verb used in each sentences. That is the preamble of what concord actually means.
Before we continue it is necessary to list out most of the auxilliary verbs frequently used both in their singular and plural form so as to facilitate our learning.

Singular Plural
is are
was were
has have
does do

Singular verb

A singular verb has 's'added to it such as;Writes,plays,runs,etc. From the image above,the boy is performing an action ie,he is singing(a verb) how can we construct a sentence that involves the boy and the action he is performing.

Examples

  • He sings very well.
  • She dances well.
  • She eats fast.
  • She goes to the mosque

Plural verb

A plural verb has no 's' added to it and it is usually used when we have a plural noun or pronoun (two or more persons).such verbs include;write,play,run,etc.In order to make a correct sentence,plural noun must be used with a plural verb.

Examples

  • They sing very well.
  • They eat very fast.
  • They go to the mosque.

RULES OF CONCORD

Rule 1:

When a conjunction other than 'and'is used to link two subject,the verb agrees with the first subject of the sentence.
Examples of conjunctions other than 'and' are; with,together-with,as-well-as,along-with,no-less-than,including,in-collaboration-with,not-to-mention,etc

Examples:

  1. Jane with her husband is here now.
  2. Sadatu as well as the maids likes plaintain.
  3. The teacher with his wife is here.
  4. The director no less than his workers is to blame

Note the verbs;is and likes used in the sentences above are singular verb

Rule 2:

When the conjunction "and" is used to join two subjects a plural verb is used.Remember in rule one, two subjects were used and a singular verb was used because the conjunction used was not "and".
Note; when "and" is used the verb MUST be pluralized because it refers to two different people

Examples

  1. Jane and her husband are here now.
  2. Sadiku and the maids like plaintain.

Exception

When "and" is used to join two singular subjects which refer to the same person and thing , a singular verb is used.
Note;this occurs in the abscent of the definite article"The"before the second subject.

Examples

  1. The player and coach is in the stadium.

Note; A Singular verb "is"was used in the example above because the two subject is being referred to one person(ie with the absent of "the" on the second subject).

  1. The Emir and Conqueror of the territories arrives next week.

Note; The Emir is the Conqueror so the two subject is actually referring to one person,hence the singular verb "arrives" is used.

Take cognizance:

When "the" is added to the second subject, the two singular subjects do no longer refer to the same person or thing , then it will take a plural "verb".

Example:

  1. The player and the coach are in the stadium.

    2. Note; A plural verb "are" is used because the sentence is referring to two different people,and there is a repetition of the definite article"the".

Rule 3:

In this rule we will be talking about Proximity-concord,the word "closeness" can be used in place of proximity for understanding.Looking at the image above you can see how close those people are, the same applies for proximity concord. When two different subjects are linked using "either or","neither nor ",no-one-else,etc.The verb agrees with with the subject closer to it.

Examples:

  • Neither clifford nor his parents attend the meeting now.

Note; The plural verb "attend" is used because the verb agrees with the subject closer to it(parents) which is plural,ignoring the singular subject (clifford) farther from it.

  • In my opinion,neither the players nor the coach deserves praise for the result of the match.

Note; The singular verb "deserves"is used because the verb agrees with the singular subject (coach) close to it , ignoring the plural subject(players) farther from it.And this happens whenever "either or","neither or",etc is used.

Take cognizance:

When "either" or "neither" is used in a sentence the verb to be used must be singular.

Rule 4:

In this rule agreement of the verb is determined by the noun that comes before the relative pronoun "who", "which", "that",etc.

Examples

  • We all pitied the man who was severely injured.

Note;The verb agrees with is the noun immediately after the relative pronoun(for sentence that contain relative pronoun).

  • Joe is one of the boys who are clever.

Note; The noun that comes before the relative pronoun is plural (boys) therefore a plural noun must agree with a plural verb.

Exceptions:

when "the only one of the" is present ,here the verb must be singular irrespective of the noun or pronoun that comes before the relative pronoun

Example

  • Joe is the only one of the boys who is clever.

Take a close look at the sentence, the noun that comes before a relative pronoun is supposed to determine the verb but in this case that is ignored because of the present of "the only one of the" in the sentence.

2

When starting a sentence with "one of" the last verb of the sentence must be singular in agreement with "one" which is also singular.

Examples

  • One of the rioters who were arrested yesterday has come.

The last verb is singular(has) it agrees with "one of"(which is also singular),the plural verb "were" agrees with the noun before the relative pronoun which is plural as well(rioters).

Rule 5:

When "one of" is used to begin a sentence,it is always followed by a plural noun or pronoun and a singular verb

Examples

  • "one of" my friends is here.

This one is straight-forward,when "one of" is used it is followed by a plural noun (friends) and a singular verb(is).

  • One of the vice-presidents acts in an interim capacity.

According to the rule when "one of" begins a sentence it is followed by a plural noun(Vice-Presidents) and a singular verb (acts;Remember,singular verbs have "s")

Rule 6:

When we begin a sentence with a definite article"the" followed by an adjective such as ; rich, poor, blind, priviledge handicapped,young,etc.Then a plural verb will be used after them.

Examples

  • The rich also cry.

"The" is a definite article,followed by an adjective (rich), hence the verb that agrees will be plural(Cry).

  • The poor deserve financial assistance.

Definite article plus adjective agree with a plural verb.

Rule 7:

A number of is followed by a plural verb while The number of is followed by a singular verb.

Examples

  • A number of students have missed the opportunity to re-register for their courses.
  • The police claim that a number of stolen cars have been recovered.
  • The number of cars in the car park has increased dramatically.

Rule 8:

The expression many a/an is followed by a singular noun or pronoun and a singular verb.
Many a/an=singular noun +singular verb.

Examples

  • Many a student likes going on holiday lesson

From the rule above, when ever "many a/an" is used in a sentence it is followed by a singular noun (Student) and a singular verb (likes).

  • Many a candidate fails to realise the difference between written and spoken English.

Rule 9:

when the expression "more than" is used as the subject of a sentence, the verb to be used is determined by the noun or pronoun that comes after it

  • More than two players were given a red card.

When ever "more than" is used as the subject, the verb to be used is determined by the noun(either a singular or a plural noun) that comes after "more than".

  • More than one student has paid the fee.

Rule 10:

When Each and Every is used as the subject of a sentence,it must be followed by a "singular noun" and a "singular verb".

Examples

  • Every woman loves her children.

when ever every or each is used ,it is followed by a singular noun as in "woman"and a singular verb as in "loves".

  • Each player was rewarded.

B

Same rule applies to singular subjects that are joined using "and".

Examples

  • Every fork,spoon and knife has to be counted.
  • Each player and coach was rewarded.

C

When "Each of" , "Everyone of" ,"neither of" ,"either of" is used as the subject,it is followed by a plural noun and a singular verb.

Example:

  • We are not suprised he was not elected every one of his constituents knows that he is unreliable.

Exceptions

1

When "each ,and "every" come after the plural subject or compound subject , then a plural verb is used.

Example

  • The player and the coach each were rewarded.

You can clearly see that each of the subjects talking about two different people come before the word "each" therefore the verb to be used is a plural verb.

2

When "both" or "both of" is used as the subject of a sentence, it is followed by a plural noun and plural verb.

Example

  • Both the boys and the girls are here.
  • Both of you should come here.

    • Rule 11:

    When a fraction or a percentage refers to a singular word , a singular verb is used .When it refers to a plural word a plural verb is used.

    Examples

    • Ninety percent of the house is neat.
    • Ninety percent of the houses are neat.
    • Three quarters of the people in the village were killed but only half of their huts were affected.
    • Three quarters of the hostel has been painted and three quarters of the students have moved in .
    • Two percent of the unskilled workers are retained.

    Rule 12:

    When unit of measurements , money , distance or time is used as subject of a sentence, a singular verb is always used though the unit look plural in appearance this unit include;

    • measurements;grammes, yards, litres,etc.
    • Money;Pounds,Naira,shillings,dollars,etc.
    • Distance; metres, hours, years, etc.
    • Time; days, hours, years,etc.

    Examples

    • Fifty litres is enough to fill the tank of the car.
    • Four years is too short for their break up.
    • Four weeks is enough for the police to complete their investigation.
